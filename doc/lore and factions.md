# Lore and Facitons

There are two factions featured in the game: the U.R.A., the good guys, and the Pirates, the bad guys. At least, that's what the U.R.A. claims in its glossy advertising.

Perhaps a future multiplayer mode will allow for the creation of multiple factions and alliances?

## U.R.A. (Unified Resource Authority)

After a long period of wars, three major mining powers decided to join forces to create a galaxy where order reigns and resource trade is "profitable" (not really).

They occupy the most densely populated areas of the galaxy, particularly the center. That's where the best resources are found, including uranium.

### The Facade (Propaganda):

"Unified" evokes peace, the end of faction wars, and the pooling of resources for humanity's survival in space. This is their official narrative.

The entire GUI is designed with U.R.A. branding in mind, as the player is, in a way, part of it.

### The Reality (Control):

"Unified" primarily signifies monopoly. If a resource is unified, it is centralized. Whoever possesses the resource possesses life.

**The Uranium Reference:**

The Uranium analogy suggests that the faction founded its empire on a primary energy resource. This is why they dislike it when "U.R.A." is written without periods.

U.R.A. has "secured" all the Uranium deposits at the center of the galaxy, forcing others to go through their "Registries" to obtain them.

Uranium must be refined to be used as an energy source. Naturally, the U.R.A. forbids the purchase or construction of refineries. The law states that "refining uranium yourself is a deadly risk." Officially, this is because "uranium refining is extremely dangerous to health and requires expert work," but in reality, the real danger lies in what the U.R.A. will do if they catch you refining uranium without going through their expensive system.

### Trading

The player can trade with U.R.A. stations, with purchase prices much higher than resale prices. This is also the primary way to obtain certain resources such as refined uranium or advanced technologies (photovoltaic panels, capacitors, etc.).

Money is in the form of "credits."

## The Pirates

They prefer the term "Independents." They are technically criminals because they steal or mine illegally, but they do so to escape a system that deprives them of their freedom and barely pays them enough to buy bread.

They occupy the edge of the galaxy, where resources are rare but so is U.R.A. They dislike outsiders, being overly suspicious, and will not hesitate to shoot.

Desperately short of resources, and with the U.R.A. monopolozing the best mining places, they do not hesitate to attack ships transporting rare resources. The player is therefore likely to have some unpleasant encounters.

## Player Story

Upon first launching the game, the terminal is open with the U.R.A. operating system and a log: _"Administering 50mg of Neuro-Uranitol for synaptic stabilization following a Class 4 traumatic brain injury."_

Next, the logs would indicate the initialization of the escape pod bound for "[system error]".

The terminal then changes to a blurry view, like someone waking up, of a U.R.A. station in a wide shot. The view transitions to an internal view showing the pod being positioned and then ejected.

After a fade to black, the player wakes up to a vision of the pod crashed on a planet. They don't know it, in fact, they know nothing at all, but they are on the most distant planet in the galaxy.

## The Law and Consequences

If the player breaks the law, the U.R.A. will apply sanctions by 3 Classes:

- **Class 1**: Minor offenses, such as illegal mining, minor theft or travelling in restricted areas. Consequences include fines and temporary restrictions on trading.
- **Class 2**: More serious offenses, such as attacking U.R.A. ships or stealing uranium. Consequences include being temporarily banned from U.R.A. stations, preventing trading and repairs, and being hunted by U.R.A. patrols.
- **Class 3**: High treason, such as destroying a U.R.A. station. Consequences include being permanently banned from the U.R.A.

The player **does not know** about laws (because of memory loss) and will have to discover them through trial and error. Warning signs and status information will be provided through the terminal.

If permanently banned from U.R.A., the player will be approached by pirates offering to join them, which will allow access to their stations and resources, but at the cost of being hunted by U.R.A. patrols.

### Becoming a Pirate

If the player chooses to join the pirates, they will have access to their stations and resources. Prices will be more reasonable, but ressources like uranium will be more difficult to obtain. At least no one will stop them from refining uranium on their own.

### Refusing to Join the Pirates

If the player refuses to join the pirates, they will have to survive on their own, which will be difficult but not impossible. They will have to rely on their skills and ingenuity to find resources and survive in a hostile environment. This is the most challenging path.
