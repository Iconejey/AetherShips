# Racks

Rack blocks allow a ship or other mobile entities to dock with another entity. All rack blocks are identical. They have no orientation or function. They all allow docking, rotation, moving along connected rack blocks, and taking off.

A ship docked to a rack on the ship's layer 0 will be considered physically part of layer 1. On a rack on layer 1, it will be considered part of layer 2. And on a rack on layer 2, it will be considered above the ship (as if there were a layer 3).

## Examples of rack usage:

- A small ship on a large ship, a station or a planet.
- Drones and turrets that are part of a bigger entity.
- Moving parts.
- Containers.

We can imagine a small mining drone coming back to the mother ship to deliver its full container. The drone then goes back to the docks with the others while the container gets routed to the storage room.
